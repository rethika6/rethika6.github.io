About Computational Media

What is it?

Computational Media is the intersection of Computer Science and Design.

Courses

Computational Media majors take all of the Computer Science general education requirements. The upper level courses are focused on certain threads. I chose to take Artificial Intelligence and Interaction Design. One half of my coursework is with the college of Computing and the other is with the LMC School.

Interests

My interests include UI/UX design, mobile app development, web development, artificial intelligence, and human-computer interaction.

Future

The Computational Media major gives me the tools to work in many different fields. Some examples include working as a software engineer, mobile or web developer, product manager, or a UI/UX designer.

About Me

Hi! My name is Rethika Ambalam and I am a sophomore at the Georgia Institute of Technology. This is my first year at Tech as I transferred from the University of Tennessee in Knoxville.
The main reason I came to Tech was because I wanted to major in Compuational Media. This major allows me to study the intersection of technology and design. I have had an interest in design since I was litte. In my free time, I would learn how to make duct tape wallets and jewelry. I was entranced by crafting. Everything I did was always meticulous and measured. I followed youtube tutorials closely to the point where I now realize I was iterating through the steps and thinking about crafting through a computational lense. All of these experiences led me to where I am now.
Some of my hobbies include playing tennis, photography, and playing board games with friends and family. Fun fact, I took the photo you see on the main page. Furthermore, I love traveling, watching Formula 1 and watching TV. My current favorite TV show is Suits and my favorite movie is Now You See Me!
